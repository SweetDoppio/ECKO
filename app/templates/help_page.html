Glossary

A

Administrative Security: Precautions taken to manage and mitigate risk within an organization, such as implementing policies and procedures.
Anti-Malware: Software designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software (malware) from computer systems and networks.
Awareness Training: Educational programs aimed at informing employees about security risks and safe practices to protect sensitive information.

B

Biometric: Unique physical or behavioral characteristics used to identify individuals, such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or voice patterns.
Blacklist: A list of entities, such as IP addresses or domains, that are prohibited from accessing a system or network due to malicious activity.
Blue Team: The internal group responsible for defending an organization's information systems against cyber threats and attacks.

C

Cybersecurity: The practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and damage.
Credential: A set of information, such as a username and password, used to verify a user's identity and grant access to a system or account.
Cloud Security: Measures and protocols implemented to protect data and applications stored in cloud computing environments from cyber threats and unauthorized access.
